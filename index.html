About Short Stories

A long story is a piece of fiction that is typically much shorter in length compared to a novel. While there is no definitive word count that categorizes a work as a short story, most range from around 1,000 to 7,500 words.

Key Characteristics of Short Stories

  • Brevity: A short story is a concise work that focuses on a single central conflict, theme, or character.
  • Singular Focus: Unlike a novel, a short story typically has a narrower scope and concentrates on one main event, setting, or character.
  • Tight Structure: Short stories often have a tightly structured plot, with a clearly defined beginning, middle, and end.
  • Impactful Ending: Short stories aim to deliver an impactful, thought-provoking, or emotionally resonant ending.
  • Snapshot of Life: Short stories can provide a glimpse into a character's life, a specific moment in time, or a particular situation.

Famous Short Stories

  • "The Tell-Tale Heart" by Edgar Allan Poe
  • "The Lottery" by Shirley Jackson
  • "Hills Like White Elephants" by Ernest Hemingway
  • "The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas" by Ursula K. Le Guin
